
//Present continuous (I am doing) 

I am doing.
나는 하는 중입니다.

//A. Study this example situation: 

She is in her car.
그녀는 그녀의 자동차 안에 있습니다.

She is on her way to work.
그녀는 일하러 가는 길 입니다.

She is driving to work.
그녀는 일하러 가기 위해 운전중 입니다.

She is driving now.
그녀는 지금 운전하고 있습니다.

//This means: she is driving now, at the time of speaking. The action is not finished. 
//Am/is/are ~ing is the present continuous: 

I'm driving.
나 운전중 이예요.

//he/she/it
He is working.
그는 일하는중 입니다.

//we/you/they
We are doing it.
우리는 그걸 하고 있어요.

//B.  I  am  doing  something  =  I'm  in  the  middle  of  doing  something;  
//I've  started  doing  it  and  I  haven't finished yet. 
//Often the action is happening at the time of speaking: 
    
Please don't make so much noise.
제발 너무 시끄럽게 하지마.

//(not 'I work')
I'm working.
나 일하고 있어.

Where's she?
그녀는 어디에 있나요?

//(not 'she has a bath')
She's having a bath.
그녀는 목욕하고 있어요.

Let's go out now.
밖으로 나가자.

//(not 'it doesn't rain')
It isn't raining any more.
더이상 비오지 않을거야.

//(at a party)
//Hello, Jane. 
//(not 'do you enjoy')
Are you enjoying the party?
너 파티를 즐기고 있니?

I'm tired.
나 피곤해.

I'm going to bed now.
나 지금 자러 갈거야.

Goodnight!
잘자!

//But the action is not necessarily happening at the time of speaking. For example: 

We are talking in a cafe.
우리는 카페에서 이야기하고 있어요.

I'm reading an interesting book.
나는 재미있는 책을 읽고 있습니다.

I'll lend it to you when I've finished it.
내가 그걸 끝마치면 너 한테 빌려줄게.

//Tom is not reading the book at the time of speaking. 
//He means that he has started it but not finished it yet. 

He is in the middle of reading it.
그는 그것을 읽고 있는 중입니다.

//Some more examples: 
    
She wants to work in USA, so she is learning English.
그녀는 미국에서 일하는 것을 원하므로, 영어를 공부하려 합니다.
그녀는 미국에서 일하고 싶어서 영어 공부하고 있습니다.

//(but perhaps she isn't learning English exactly at the time of speaking) 

Some friends of mine are building their own house.
내 친구중에 몇명은 그들만의 집을 지으려 합니다.
내 친구 몇명은 그들만의 집을 짓고 있습니다.

They hope it will be finished before next summer.
그들은 내년 여름전에 그것이 종료되길 희망합니다.

//C. We use the present continuous when we talk about things happening in a period around now.
//(for example, today/this week/this evening etc.):

//(not 'you work hard today')
You're working hard today.
너 오늘 열심히 일하고 있구나.

I have a lot to do.
난 해야할 것이 많습니다.

Is she working this week?
그녀는 이번주에 일하나요?

she's on holiday.
그녀는 휴가중 입니다.

//We use the present continuous when we talk about changes happening around now:

//(not 'rises')
The population of the world is rising very fast.
세계 인구는 아주 빠르게 증가하고 있습니다.

//(not 'does your English get better')
Is your English getting better?
영어 실력이 점점 좋아지고 있니?

//Present continuous (I am doing) 
//Use the continuous for something that is happening at or around the time of speaking. 
//The action is not finished. 

The water is boiling.
물이 끓고 있어요.

Can you turn it off?
그것 좀 꺼 주겠니?
 
Listen to those people.
저 사람들 얘기좀 들어봐.

What language are they speaking? 
그들은 어떤 언어를 말하고 있나요?

Let's go out.
밖으로 나가자.

It isn't raining now.
지금 비가 오지 않아요.

Don't disturb me.
날 방해하지 말아요.

I'm busy.
난 바빠요.

What are you doing?
뭐하고 있니?

지금 자러 갈거야.

//Use the continuous for a temporary situation: 

I'm living with some friends until I find a flat.
나는 flat을 찾을때 까지 친구 몇명과 함께 살고 있습니다.
 
You're working hard today.
너 오늘 열심히 일하고 있구나.

I've got a lot to do.
난 할일이 많아.

I always go to work by car.
//(not 'I'm always going')
나는 항상 자동차로 출근 합니다.
 
//You can also say 'I'm always doing something', but this has a different meaning. For example: 

I've lost my key again.
나 열쇠를 또 잃어 버렸어.

I'm always losing things.
난 물건을 자주 잃어 버려.
 
//I'm always losing things' does not mean that I lose things every time. 
//It means that I lose things too often, more often than normal. 
//'You're always ~ing' means that you do something very often, more often than the speaker thinks is normal or reasonable.

You're always watching television.
넌 텔레비젼을 너무 자주 본다.
넌 항상 텔레비젼을 보고 있어.

You should do something more active.
넌 좀더 활동적인 것을 해야해.
 
He is never satisfied. 
그는 절대 만족하지 않아요.

He's always complaining. 
그는 항상 불평한다.
