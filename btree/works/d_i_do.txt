//Present simple (I do) 

I do.
나는 합니다.

//A. Study this example situation: 

He is a bus driver, but now he is in bed asleep. 
그는 버스 기사 입니다, 그러나 지금 그는 침대에서 자고 있습니다.

He is not driving a bus.
그는 버스 운전 중이 아닙니다.

He is asleep.
그는 자고 있습니다. 

He drives a bus. 
그는 버스를 운전 합니다.

He is a bus driver.
그는 버스 기사 입니다.
 
//Drive(s)/work(s)/do(es) etc. is the present simple: 
//I/we/you/they    drive/work/do etc. 
//he/she/it    drives/works/does etc. 
 
//B. We use the present simple to talk about things in general. 
//We are not thinking only about now. 
//We use it to say that something happens all the time or repeatedly, or that something is true in general. 
//It is not important whether the action is happening at the time of speaking: 
    
Nurses took after patients in hospitals. 
간호사들은 병원에서 환자들을 돌봅니다.

I usually go away at weekends.
나는 보통 주말에 멀리 갑니다.
 
The earth goes round the sun.
지구는 태양 주위를 돕니다.
 
//Remember that we say: he/she/it -s. Don't forget the s: 
//I work ... but He works ... They teach ... but My sister teaches ... 

//C. We use do/does to make questions and negative sentences: 
//do I/we/you/they work?/come?/do? 
//does he/she/it work?/come?/do? 
//I/we/you/they don't work/come/do 
//he/she/it doesn't work/come/do 

I'm from Canada. 
난 캐나다 출신 입니다.

I come from Canada.
난 캐나다에서 왔어요.

Where do you come from? 
너 어디에서 왔니?

Where are you from?
고향이 어디 입니까?
출신 지역이 어디 신가요?

Would you like a cigarette?
담배 한대 피우시겠어요?

No, thanks. 
아뇨, 괜찮아요.

I don't smoke.
난 담배를 피우지 않아요.
 
What does this word mean? 
//(not 'What means this word?')
이 단어는 무슨 의미 인가요?
 
Rice doesn't grow in cold climates. 
벼는 추운 기후에서 자라지 않아요.

//In the following examples do is also the main verb: 
What do you do?
무슨 일을 하시나요?
What's your job?
직업이 뭔가요?

I work in a shop.
난 상점에서 일합니다.
 
He's so lazy.
그는 너무 게을러요.

He doesn't do anything to help me.
//(not 'He doesn't anything') 
그는 나를 돕기 위해 하는 것이 아무것도 없어요.

//D. We use the present simple when we say how often we do things: 

I get up at 8 o'clock every morning. 
//(not 'I'm getting')
나는 매일 아침 8시에 일어납니다.
 
How often do you go to the dentist? 
//(not 'How often are you going?') 
얼마나 자주 치과에 가나요?

She doesn't drink tea very often.
그녀는 차를 그렇게 자주 마시지 않아요.
 
In summer he usually plays tennis once or twice a week. 
여름에 그는 일주일에 한두번 테니스를 칩니다.

//E. I promise/I apologise etc. 
//Sometimes we do things by saying something. 
//For example, when you promise to do something, you can say  'I  promise  ...';  
//when  you  suggest  something,  you  can  say  I suggest  ...'.  
//We  use  the  present  simple (promise/suggest etc.) in sentences like this: 

I promise I won't be late. 
//(not 'I'm promising') 
늦지 않는다고 약속할게.
    
What do you suggest I do?
//I suggest that you ... 
내가 무엇을 해야 할지 제안 좀 해주시겠어요?
    
//In the same way we say: I apologise .../I advise .../I insist .../I agree ... /I refuse ... etc. 

Water boils at 100 degrees celsius.
물은 섭씨 100도 에서 끓습니다.
 
Excuse me.
실례합니다.

Do you speak English?
영어를 사용 하시나요?

It doesn't rain very much in winter.
겨울에는 비가 매우 많이 오지 않습니다.
 
What do you usually do at weekends?
주말에 보통 무엇을 하시나요?
 
What do you do?
무슨일을 하시나요?

What's your job?
직업이 뭔가요?
 
I always go to bed before midnight.
나는 항상 자정 이전에 잠자리로 갑니다.
 
Most people learn to swim when they are children.
대부분의 사람들은 어릴때 수영을 배웁니다.

//Use the simple for a permanent situation: 

My parents live in London.
내 부모님은 런던에 삽니다.

They have lived there all their lives.
그들은 평생을 거기에 살았습니다.
 
He isn't lazy.
그는 게으르지 않습니다.

He works very hard most of the time.
그는 대체적으로 매우 열심히 일합니다.


//Present continuous and present simple (2) (I am doing and I do)
//A. We use continuous tenses only for actions and happenings (they are eating/it is raining etc.).
//Some verbs (for example, know and like) are not action verbs. 
//You cannot say 'I am knowing' or ,they are liking'; you can only say 'I know', 'they like'. 
//The following verbs are not normally used in continuous tenses: 

//like    love    hate    want    need    prefer    know    realise    suppose    mean    
//understand    believe   remember    belong    contain    consist    depend    seem 

I'm hungry.
나 배고파.
I want something to eat.
//(not 'I'm wanting')
나 뭐좀 먹고 싶어.

Do you understand what I mean?
너 내 의도를 이해 하니?

She doesn't seem very happy at the moment.
그녀는 지금 행복해 보이지 않아요.

//When think means 'believe', do not use the continuous: 
What do you think will happen?
//(not 'what are you thinking') 
무엇이 발생할 것 같니?

You look serious.
너 심각해 보인다.
What are you thinking about?
너 무엇을 생각하고 있니?
What is going on in your mind?
무엇을 생각하고 있나요?

I'm thinking of giving up my job.
//(= I am considering)
내 직업을 포기하는걸 고려 중이야.

//When have means 'possess' etc., do not use the continuous (see Unit 17):
We're enjoying our holiday.
우리는 휴가를 즐겁게 보내고 있습니다.
We have a nice room in the hotel.
//(not 'we're having') 
우리는 호텔에서 좋은 방을 잡았어요.
We're having a great time.
우리는 멋진 시간을 보내고 있어요.

//B. See    hear    smell    taste 
//We normally use the present simple (not continuous) with these verbs: 
Do you see that man over there?
//(not 'are you seeing')
저기의 저 남자를 보고 있니?

This room smells.
이 방은 냄새 난다.
Let's open a window.
창문 좀 열자.

//We often use can + see/hear/smell/taste: 
Listen!
들어봐!
Can you hear something?
뭔가 들리지 않니?
너 뭔가를 들을 수 있니?

//But  you  can  use  the  continuous  with  see  (I'm  seeing)  
//when  the  meaning  is  'having  a  meeting  with (especially in the future--see Unit 19A): 

I'm seeing the manager tomorrow morning.
나 내일 아침에 팀장님을 만날거야.
나 내일 아침에 팀장님과 미팅이 있어.

//C. He is selfish and He is being selfish 
//The present continuous of be is I am being/he is being/you are being etc. 
//I'm being = 'I'm behaving/I'm acting'. Compare: 
I can't understand why he's being so selfish.
나는 그가 왜 그렇게 이기적으로 해동 하는지 이해할 수가 없어.
//He isn't usually like that. (being selfish = behaving selfishly at the moment) 

He never thinks about other people.
그는 다른 사람을 배려하지 않는다.
He is very selfish.
//(not 'he is being') (= he is selfish generally, not only at the moment)
그는 너무 이기적이다.

//We use am/is/are being to say how somebody is behaving. It is not usually possible in other sentences: 
It's hot today.
//(not 'it is being hot') 
오늘은 덮다.

She is very tired.
//(not 'is being tired') 
그녀는 너무 피곤하다.

//D. Look and feet 
//You can use the present simple or continuous when you say how somebody looks or feels now: 
You look well today.
너 오늘 좋아 보여.
You're looking well today.
너는 오늘 좋아 보인다.

How do you feel now?
지금 기분 어때?
How are you feeling now?
지금 기분이 어떠니?

I usually feel tired in the morning.
//(not 'I'm usually feeling')
나는 보통 아침에 피곤해.
